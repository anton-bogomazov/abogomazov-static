Inject'em all kata

23 February 2024
di, solid, engineering, ood

The purpose of this exercise is to dispel the magic of DI frameworks and grow understanding of Dependency Injection (DI) as a concept.

Early in my career, I read Clean Architecture by Robert Martin and discovered the world of architecture and design. It wasn’t easy to start thinking outside of layered architectures at first. I quickly realised how to make a database a detail, a little later - web APIs. But it took me a while to figure out how to make Spring a detail.

The backends I encountered were definitely Spring-centric. It seemed like everything would fall into disrepair without it. Now I understand this is a symptom of a tight coupling with framework.

Finally, I put that thought aside and continued labelling my components as @Service and @Controller. But one day I came across Mark Seemann’s book “Dependency Injection” and was finally able to write a core application module that didn’t depend on Spring.

I tried to share this knowledge with people I had mentored in one of my previous companies. Immediately I found that it was as difficult for people to accept the application without Spring invasion as it was for me at the time.

Today I thought of a way to make learning DI a bit more fun. I like to solve coding katas and came up with one about DI.

I doubt it’s perfect, so I’d love to hear your feedback and improve it: https://github.com/anton-bogomazov/inject-em-all-kata
